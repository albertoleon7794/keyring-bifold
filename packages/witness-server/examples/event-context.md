# Digital Identity Symposium - April 2026

Welcome to the Digital Identity Symposium, a gathering of researchers, practitioners, and policymakers exploring the future of decentralized digital identity.

## Event Details

- **When**: April 16, 2026
- **Where**: Berkman Klein Center at Harvard Law School, Cambridge, MA
- **Hosted by**: Berkman Klein Center for Internet & Society

## About This Event

Digital identity is often controlled by institutions or platforms, but what if individuals could own and manage their own credentials?

**The Digital Identity Symposium convenes researchers, technologists, practitioners and end-user representatives to explore this shift toward user-owned identity**. The event will feature the public launch of **Keyring** (previously known as the ASML Wallet), an open-source application that demonstrates how individuals can take control of their digital identity.

Through a live product demonstration and a forward-looking panel discussion, **the symposium will examine emerging approaches, real-world applications, and pathways to adoption for user-controlled identity systems.**

### What to Expect

This symposium brings together leading voices in digital identity, verifiable credentials, and decentralized trust systems. Participants will:

- Share research findings and practical implementations
- Demonstrate cutting-edge identity technologies
- Exchange verifiable relationship credentials in person
- Participate in witnessed credential exchanges

## Witnessed Credential Exchange

At this event, we're demonstrating a novel approach to digital identity:

- **Relationship Credentials**: When you connect with someone, you can exchange verifiable credentials about your relationship
- **Witness Verification**: The Berkman Klein Center witness service verifies these exchanges happened at the event
- **Privacy-Preserving**: The witness doesn't see your private information - only that an exchange occurred
- **Verifiable Provenance**: The witnessed credentials prove you met someone at this specific event

## How to Participate

1. Install the Keyring application on your mobile device
2. Connect to the witness service by scanning the QR code
3. Meet other participants and exchange credentials
4. Your witnessed credentials will be automatically issued

## Narrative Flow

1. Motivate the need for user-owned identity systems
2. Present our work and contributions, including a live demonstration of the prototype solution
3. Facilitate a forward-looking panel discussion on the future of user-owned digital identity systems

## Forward-Looking Considerations

- Feasibility of user-owned identity in the age of AI without compromising innovation.
- Pathways to meaningful adoption of the technology.
- Privacy-preserving ways to track adoption metrics, such as usage, engagement, and sentiment.
- Examine how user-owned identity models can support individuals without official IDs to participate in online systems, including financial, social, entertainment, and government programs.
- Which use cases are likely to realize the greatest short-term value from user-owned identity systems?

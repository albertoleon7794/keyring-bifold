# Berkman Klein Center for Internet & Society

The Berkman Klein Center for Internet & Society at Harvard University is a research institute focused on studying the internet and digital technology's impact on society.

## Mission

We bring together faculty, students, fellows, entrepreneurs, and others from diverse disciplines to explore, understand, and shape cyberspace and the future of democracy, openness, and innovation.

## Key Focus Areas

- **Digital Rights & Governance**: Research on internet policy, free expression, and digital civil liberties
- **Technology & Society**: Studying how technology shapes human interaction and social structures  
- **Privacy & Security**: Exploring privacy rights, surveillance, and cybersecurity
- **Innovation & Entrepreneurship**: Supporting technology innovation that serves the public good

## Our Approach

We believe in:
- Open collaboration and knowledge sharing
- Multidisciplinary research and dialogue
- Public engagement and policy impact
- Supporting the next generation of digital leaders

## The Witness Service

As part of our commitment to advancing digital identity and trust technologies, we're operating this witness service to:
- Demonstrate practical applications of decentralized identity
- Enable verifiable relationship credentials
- Support privacy-preserving digital interactions
- Advance the state of the art in identity verification
